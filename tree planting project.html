Kwale Green Corridor Initiative

Restoring ecosystems and combating climate change through strategic reforestation

About the Project

The Kwale Green Corridor is our flagship reforestation initiative aimed at planting 10,000 native trees along critical wildlife corridors in Kwale County. This project addresses deforestation, soil erosion, and biodiversity loss while creating sustainable green spaces for communities and wildlife.

Project Objectives

  • Restore 50 hectares of degraded land through native tree planting
  • Create wildlife corridors connecting fragmented forest patches
  • Engage 500 community members in conservation activities
  • Establish community nurseries for sustainable seedling production
  • Reduce soil erosion by 60% in target areas

Success Story: Waa Community Transformation

In Waa Location, what was once barren land has been transformed into a thriving green space. Through community collaboration, we've planted over 1,200 trees that are now providing shade, improving air quality, and creating habitat for local wildlife.

"The temperature in our village has noticeably dropped since we started this project. Our children now have a green space to play, and we're seeing birds and butterflies return to our area," says Mama Aisha, a community elder.

The project has also created economic opportunities through the community nursery, where members learn seedling propagation and earn income from selling excess seedlings to neighboring communities.

Project Timeline & Milestones

January 2024

Project Launch

Official launch of Kwale Green Corridor with community stakeholders and local government representatives. Initial community mobilization and site identification.

March 2024

First Planting Season

Successfully planted 800 native trees with participation from 5 schools and 3 community groups. Established first community nursery.

June 2024

Expansion Phase

Expanded to 3 additional communities. Partnered with Fazil Chinoy Fellowship for technical support and resources.

October 2024

2,500 Trees Milestone

Celebrated planting our 2,500th tree. Documented 85% survival rate through proper aftercare and community monitoring.
